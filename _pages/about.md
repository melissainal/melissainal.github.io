I am a cellular biologist and imaging specialist dedicated to developing advanced workflows that help scientists resolve complex biological questions, validate targets, and generate quantitative data for discovery and translational research. My background spans in vivo super-resolution microscopy, tissue clearing, live imaging, assay development, and image analysis across both *Drosophila* and mammalian systems.

During my PhD at the University of Georgia (2025), I built a first-of-its-kind in vivo STORM imaging pipeline in motor neurons, achieving 20-nm lateral resolution and enabling sub-synaptic protein analysis in real time. I have also developed dual-color super-resolution workflows, mapped localization patterns for over 15 protein targets from more than 200 scRNA-seq candidates, and built robust analysis pipelines for complex, high-content imaging datasets.

I approach scientific challenges as a problem-solver, focused on building imaging infrastructure and improving experimental workflows to turn high-content data into biological insight.

### Research & Technical Expertise
*   **Imaging Modalities:** STORM, PALM, SIM, confocal microscopy, and live imaging.
*   **Methodology & Preparation:** Tissue clearing (CLARITY), primary cell isolation, and experimental design.
*   **Computational Biology:** R (Seurat), Python, ImageJ/Fiji, and multi-study data management.

### Professional Interests
I am actively pursuing roles as a Discovery, Translational, Platform, Biomarker, or Imaging Scientist in the biotech and pharmaceutical sectors, where I can apply my expertise in high-resolution imaging and transcriptomic data integration to drive research forward.
