Abstract:

Over the decades, researchers have taken advantage of Drosophila as a model organism because they are cheap to maintain with their short lifespans and easy to manipulate with diverse sets of genetic tools that have been developed. The evolutionary conservation of cellular and molecular mechanisms in Drosophila has been another incentive to investigate this organism. With the current tools, we can study this organism that has been instrumental in our current understanding of neuronal circuitry development. Here, we present a sample preparation and optical setup method to study the synaptic development using super-resolution microscopy. In summary, we describe a method that evaluates development of neurons in vitro by establishing primary neuron cultures or by directly viewing the fly embryo. Particularly, we use both the UAS-Gal4 system to selectively label neuronal membranes to trace axons, growth cones, and fine subcellular structures in a continuous manner and immunofluorescence labeling techniques to look at the localization and structures of small proteins under SMLM (single molecule localization microscopy). In a representative study, we will look at the localization of cytoskeletal protein tubulin and label the membrane in primary Drosophila neuronal culture. Additionally, we will demonstrate that the SMLM approach can also be used in situ with a similar membrane labeling technique in conjunction with immunofluorescence staining. Our strategy presents opportunities for further studies of different molecular machinery of the neurons during development or of their morphologies at different stages of growth by using single- or multi-color SMLM imaging.
